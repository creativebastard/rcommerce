## Which License Should I Choose?

### Choose AGPL-3.0 if:
- You're building a non-commercial project
- You're building an open source project
- You're a small business with limited revenue
- You're comfortable sharing your modifications
- You're self-hosting for internal use only

### Choose Commercial License if:
- You're building a proprietary/SaaS product
- You want to keep your modifications private
- You want to offer hosted R Commerce services
- You need professional support and SLA
- You want white-label rights

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I switch from AGPL to Commercial later?
Yes. You can purchase a Commercial License at any time to convert your
AGPL-licensed deployment to commercial use.

### Can I use AGPL for my SaaS?
Yes, but you must share your source code. If you want to keep your code
private, you need a Commercial License.

### What happens if I stop paying for Commercial License?
You must either:
- Purchase a new license
- Migrate to AGPL and share your source code
- Stop using the Software

### Can I contribute to R Commerce?
Yes! We welcome contributions. Contributors must sign a Contributor License
Agreement (CLA) allowing us to dual-license their contributions.

### Do I own my modifications?
Yes, you own your modifications. Under AGPL, you must share them. Under
Commercial License, you can keep them private.

### Can I sell R Commerce?
Under AGPL: No, but you can sell services around it.
Under Commercial: Yes, with white-label rights.
